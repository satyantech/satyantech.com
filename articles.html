ASTROSAT Fully Assembled - ISRO's First Mission Operated As Space Observatory Under the leadership of Mr. A S Kiran Kumar, the Chairman of Space Commission and ISRO, the organisation is gearing up for the launch of India's first ever astronomy mission named ASTROSAT, which is dedicated to the study of celestial objects. ISRO recently published on their official website that its spacecraft has been fully assembled and their plans would be executed as per schedule. Planned to be fired off in the later part of this year, this spacecraft will be onboard a PSLV C-34 when its blasted to the equatorial orbit, which is at a distance of 650 km from Earth. ASTROSAT would be conducting observations in the Ultraviolet (UV), optical, low and high energy X-ray wavebands simultaneously, once it reaches its destination. It is important to note here that this is the first mission by ISRO to be operated as a dedicated space observatory.
